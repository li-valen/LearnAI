"AI is more than ChatGPT. Its a mathematically complex set of networks that make thousands of calculations per second." - Daniel Bourke

Whats Behind AI?

Behind AI lies a complex framework of computational algorithms and data structures, primarily driven by machine learning techniques such as neural networks. Neural networks mimic the structure and function of the human brain, composed of interconnected nodes organized in layers. Through layers of processing, these networks learn from data patterns to make predictions or decisions without explicit programming. They rely heavily on vast datasets for training and fine-tuning, enabling them to recognize patterns, derive insights, and adapt to new information. Interdisciplinary collaboration across computer science, mathematics, and cognitive psychology further fuels the development of neural networks, advancing their application across diverse domains.

Image Classification

An image classification model is built upon a convolutional neural network (CNN) trained on labeled image data, optimizing its parameters through algorithms like stochastic gradient descent. Validation ensures its generalization, while evaluation on a separate test set measures its performance before deployment. Techniques like fine-tuning and regularization enhance accuracy and prevent overfitting, culminating in a reliable classification system.

Object Detection

Object detection models use specialized layers added to convolutional neural networks (CNNs) to find and classify objects in images. During training, they learn to draw boxes around objects and label them using optimization methods like stochastic gradient descent. After training, the model is tested to see how well it can find and classify objects. Once it works well, it's turned into a usable format for real-world applications. Techniques like transfer learning help improve its accuracy, ensuring it can find objects accurately and quickly in different situations.
