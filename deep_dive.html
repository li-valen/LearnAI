Whats Behind AI?

AI operates through intricate computational algorithms, predominantly powered by neural networks, mirroring the human brain's structure. These networks, comprised of interconnected nodes arranged in layers, learn from data patterns to predict and decide autonomously. They depend on extensive datasets for training and refinement, facilitating pattern recognition, insights derivation, and adaptation. Collaboration across computer science, mathematics, and cognitive psychology enhances neural networks' development and broadens their applicability.

Image Classification

CNNs revolutionize visual recognition tasks through their hierarchical approach, which begins with convolution for feature extraction, followed by pooling for dimensionality reduction. The features are then passed through fully connected layers, enabling the network to learn complex relationships within the data. This sequential process empowers CNNs to recognize patterns with remarkable accuracy across diverse domains, from image classification to object detection and beyond.

Object Detection

Object detection models use specialized layers added to convolutional neural networks (CNNs) to find and classify objects in images. During training, they learn to draw boxes around objects and label them using optimization methods like stochastic gradient descent. After training, the model is tested to see how well it can find and classify objects. Once it works well, it's turned into a usable format for real-world applications. Techniques like transfer learning help improve its accuracy, ensuring it can find objects accurately and quickly in different situations.
